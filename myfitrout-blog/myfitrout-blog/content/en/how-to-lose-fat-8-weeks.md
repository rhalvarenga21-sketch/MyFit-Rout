Losing fat is one of the most common fitness goals, but also one of the most misunderstood. In this complete guide, we'll show you exactly what to do over 8 weeks to transform your body in a healthy and sustainable way.

## What Science Says About Fat Loss

To lose fat, you need to create a **caloric deficit** — meaning you burn more calories than you consume. The general rule is a deficit of 300 to 500 calories per day, which results in approximately 0.5 kg (1 lb) of fat lost per week.

Important: very aggressive deficits (above 1000 calories) cause muscle loss, extreme fatigue, and yo-yo effect.

## The 3 Pillars of Success

### 1. Smart Nutrition

You don't need to eliminate food groups. The secret is balance:

- **Protein**: 0.7 to 1g per pound of bodyweight — preserves muscle mass
- **Carbohydrates**: Reduce, don't eliminate — your main source of energy
- **Healthy fats**: Olive oil, avocado, nuts — essential for hormones

### 2. Consistent Training

Combining **weight training** with **moderate cardio** is the most efficient formula:

- 3 to 4 days of weight training per week
- 2 to 3 cardio sessions (brisk walking, cycling, swimming)
- Adequate rest — your body transforms while you recover

### 3. Sleep and Recovery

Sleeping less than 7 hours per night increases cortisol (the stress hormone), which promotes abdominal fat storage. Prioritize 7 to 9 hours of quality sleep.

## The 8-Week Plan

**Weeks 1-2**: Adaptation — start with a 200-300 calorie deficit and establish your training routine.

**Weeks 3-5**: Acceleration — increase the deficit to 400-500 calories and add an extra cardio session.

**Weeks 6-8**: Consolidation — maintain the pace, adjust based on results, and focus on consistency.

## Expected Results

With this plan, you can expect to lose between **3 to 5 kg (6-11 lbs) of fat** over 8 weeks while maintaining muscle mass. Results vary based on starting point and consistency.

## Conclusion

Losing fat isn't about extreme diets or endless hours of cardio. It's about creating sustainable habits, training intelligently, and giving your body the rest it deserves.
