Protein is the most important nutrient for anyone looking to build muscle. But how much protein do you actually need per day? The answer is simpler than you think.

## The basic formula

The science is clear: for muscle gain, the ideal range is **1.6g to 2.2g of protein per kg of bodyweight** per day.

**Practical example:**
- Weight: 75kg (165 lbs)
- Minimum protein: 75 × 1.6 = **120g/day**
- Maximum protein: 75 × 2.2 = **165g/day**

## The best protein sources

### Animal sources (most complete):
- Chicken breast: 31g protein / 100g
- Canned tuna: 26g protein / 100g
- Eggs: 6g protein / egg
- Cottage cheese: 11g protein / 100g
- Greek yogurt: 10g protein / 100g

### Plant-based sources:
- Chickpeas: 19g protein / 100g
- Lentils: 9g protein / 100g
- Tofu: 8g protein / 100g
- Edamame: 11g protein / 100g

## How to spread it throughout the day

Research shows your body absorbs protein better when spread across 4-5 meals of 25-40g each, rather than all at once.

**Example for 75kg (goal: 130g/day):**
- Breakfast: 3 eggs + Greek yogurt = 30g
- Snack: 1 scoop protein powder = 25g
- Lunch: 150g chicken = 47g
- Dinner: 150g tuna = 39g
- **Total: ~141g ✅**

## Do you need protein supplements?

Protein powder isn't mandatory — it's just a convenient way to hit your daily goal. If you can reach your target through whole foods, you don't need supplements.

## Conclusion

Multiply your weight (kg) by 1.8 and you have a simple number to start with. Spread it across 4 meals and prioritize quality sources.
